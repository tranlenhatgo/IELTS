# True / False / Not Given Method

Skill: Reading  
Question type: True / False / Not Given  
Source: user-provided image

## Examiner Mindset

Find the `trap keyword` first. This is the word or phrase designed to make the statement look close to the text while changing the meaning.

## Method

| Step | Action | Band 8 Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identify the trap keyword in the statement. | Watch qualifiers, comparison, quantity, time, cause, and certainty. |
| 2 | Find the matching area in the text. | Do not answer from topic similarity alone. |
| 3 | Test the trap keyword against the text. | The answer depends on whether the text contradicts it, supports it, or never addresses it. |

## False vs Not Given

| Answer | Condition | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| `False` | The text mentions the trap keyword idea and contradicts it. | clear contrast, opposite meaning, incompatible limit |
| `Not Given` | The text does not address the trap keyword idea. | topic appears, but the exact claim is missing |

## Trap Keyword Types

| Type | Watch For |
|---|---|
| Quantity | all, most, some, few, only, no |
| Time | always, now, currently, previously, first, after |
| Certainty | will, may, might, likely, proven |
| Comparison | more than, less than, better, same, different |
| Cause | because, leads to, results from, due to |
| Scope | in every case, for children, in cities, worldwide |

## Quick Rule

`False` = the text fights the trap keyword.  
`Not Given` = the text never touches the trap keyword.

## Quick Example

Text:

`The museum offers free entry to students on weekdays.`

| Statement | Answer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| The museum offers free entry to all visitors on weekdays. | `False` | `all visitors` contradicts `students`. |
| The museum offers free entry to students on weekends. | `False` | `weekends` contradicts `weekdays`. |
| The museum gives students a guided tour after entry. | `Not Given` | `guided tour` is not mentioned. |
