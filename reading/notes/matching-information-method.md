# Matching Information Method

Skill: Reading  
Question type: Matching information  
Source: user-provided image

## Position In Reading

Do this question type after easier/localized question types when possible. Matching information can force full-passage scanning and wastes time if done too early.

## Method

| Step | Action | Band 8 Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Split each statement into two keyword types: general keyword and specific keyword. | Do not treat every noun as equal. Separate broad idea from hard evidence. |
| 2 | Read paragraphs in order from top to bottom. | Break long paragraphs into 2-sentence chunks. Highlight, then compare immediately. |
| 3 | Compare paragraph keywords with both keyword types in each statement. | Match meaning and evidence, not repeated wording. |
| 4 | Lock the answer only when both conditions are met. | Specific details are synonymous, and the paragraph gives evidence for the general idea. |

## Two-Keyword System

| Keyword Type | Role | Example Focus |
|---|---|---|
| General keyword | Main idea of the statement | process, problem, benefit, reason, result |
| Specific keyword | Concrete evidence to confirm the match | date, person, place, object, number, action |

## Answer Lock Conditions

Only choose a paragraph when:

- the specific details in the statement and text match by meaning
- the paragraph contains concrete evidence for the statement's general idea

## Trap

Even after finding one answer in a paragraph, finish reading the paragraph. It may contain another answer, especially when the instruction says:

`You may use any letter more than once.`

## Quick Rule

General idea tells you where to suspect. Specific evidence tells you when to answer.
