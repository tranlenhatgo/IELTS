# One-Week IELTS General Training Plan

Start date: 2026-06-29  
End date: 2026-07-05  
Test type: IELTS General Training

## Priority Order

1. Writing Task 2: highest risk because structure, ideas, grammar, and timing all matter.
2. Writing Task 1 letters: fast score gain if tone, purpose, bullets, and format are controlled.
3. Reading: improve timing and avoid traps, especially in Section 3.
4. Listening: daily practice for attention, spelling, plurals, and distractors.
5. Speaking: daily short practice with feedback and correction.

## Daily Baseline

Do these every day:

- Listening: 30 minutes timed practice plus mistake review.
- Reading: 45 to 60 minutes timed practice.
- Writing: one Task 1 or Task 2 under time pressure, then rewrite the weak parts.
- Speaking: 15 to 20 minutes answer practice, recorded if possible.
- Mistakes: update one mistake file before stopping.

## Day 1: Setup And Diagnosis

- Do one timed Listening section or full test.
- Do one timed GT Reading section or full test.
- Write one Task 1 letter in 20 minutes.
- Write one Task 2 essay plan only, then one body paragraph.
- Record answers for 5 Part 1 Speaking questions.
- Create mistake notes for the top 5 problems.

## Day 2: Writing Task 1 Letters

- Review formal, semi-formal, and informal letter tone.
- Write two letters in 20 minutes each.
- Check each letter for all three bullet points.
- Rewrite only the opening, closing, and weakest paragraph.
- Do 30 minutes Listening focused on spelling, numbers, and plurals.
- Do 30 minutes Reading focused on quick answers in easier sections.

## Day 3: Writing Task 2 Structure

- Review opinion, discussion, advantage/disadvantage, and problem/solution structures.
- Write one full Task 2 essay in 40 minutes.
- Mark thesis, topic sentences, examples, and conclusion.
- Rewrite the introduction and one weak body paragraph.
- Record one Part 2 cue card answer.
- Do one timed Reading passage.

## Day 4: Reading Speed And Accuracy

- Do a full GT Reading test under time pressure.
- Review every wrong answer and write the exact reason.
- Drill True/False/Not Given or matching questions if they caused problems.
- Write one Task 1 letter.
- Do 20 minutes Speaking Part 3 practice.

## Day 5: Listening And Speaking Control

- Do a full Listening test under time pressure.
- Review errors by type: spelling, plural, distractor, missed paraphrase, or lost focus.
- Record one full Speaking mock: Part 1, Part 2, and Part 3.
- Write short feedback on fluency, grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation.
- Write one Task 2 essay plan and one body paragraph.

## Day 6: Full Writing Practice

- Write Task 1 in 20 minutes.
- Write Task 2 in 40 minutes.
- Review both immediately.
- Rewrite the weakest 120 to 180 words from the two answers.
- Do 30 minutes light Listening or Reading practice.
- Review all mistake notes.

## Day 7: Final Review

- Do not overload.
- Review letter formats and Task 2 structures.
- Review common spelling errors and plural mistakes.
- Review Reading trap notes.
- Speak for 15 minutes on familiar topics.
- Prepare test-day checklist.

## Final Two-Day Rule

Stop learning new systems. Use only:

- Timed practice
- Mistake review
- Rewrites
- Sleep and test-day preparation
