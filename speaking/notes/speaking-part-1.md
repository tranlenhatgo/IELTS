# Speaking Part 1

Skill: Speaking
Part: Part 1
Source: user-provided image

## 3S Formula

Use when the question is difficult or too specialized.

| Step | Purpose | Move |
|---|---|---|
| Straight no | Admit limited knowledge/experience. | Do not fake expertise. |
| Switch | Move to a familiar nearby angle. | Connect the topic to daily life, people, habits, or opinion. |
| Speculation | Add a future guess or general prediction. | Show range without needing expert vocabulary. |

## Sentence Frames

| Step | Useful Patterns |
|---|---|
| Straight no | `To be honest, I don't know much about...`; `I haven't had much experience with...`; `It's not something I deal with often.` |
| Switch | `But if I think about it in daily life...`; `What I can say is...`; `It reminds me of...`; `A more familiar example would be...` |
| Speculation | `I suppose it might become more common...`; `I imagine people will...`; `It will probably depend on...`; `In the future, this could...` |

## Example

Question: `Do you enjoy going to art galleries or museums?`

Answer:

`To be honest, I don't go to art galleries or museums very often, so I wouldn't say I know much about them. But I do enjoy places that are quiet and visually interesting, especially when they show objects from local history or everyday life. I suppose I would visit them more in the future if the exhibitions were more interactive and easier to understand.`

## Band 8 Focus

- Do not answer with only `I don't know`.
- Switch smoothly; do not change the topic randomly.
- Use speculation to extend the answer, not to avoid the question.
- Keep Part 1 answers controlled: direct, developed, but not a Part 2 speech.

## Quick Rule

No expertise -> familiar angle -> future guess.
