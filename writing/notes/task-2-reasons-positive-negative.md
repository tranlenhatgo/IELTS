# Task 2 Reasons + Positive / Negative Development

Skill: Writing
Part: Task 2 essay
Question type: `What are the reasons for this? Is it a positive or negative development?`

## Position

This is a two-part question.

Body 1 answers why the trend happens.
Body 2 judges whether it is positive or negative.

## Structure

| Paragraph | Job |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase trend + give overall judgement. |
| Body 1 | Explain two main reasons for the trend. |
| Body 2 | Evaluate the result: positive, negative, or mixed. |
| Conclusion | Restate reasons + judgement. |

## Fixed Frames

Introduction:

`In many places, [trend] has become more common. This is mainly due to [reason 1] and [reason 2], and I believe it is largely a [positive/negative] development.`

Body 1:

- `One major reason is that...`
- `Another factor is...`
- `As a result, people are more likely to...`

Body 2:

- `I consider this development mostly positive/negative because...`
- `This can lead to...`
- `Although [minor opposite point], the overall effect is still [positive/negative].`

Conclusion:

`In conclusion, [trend] is mainly caused by [reason 1] and [reason 2]. Overall, I see it as [judgement] because [main effect].`

## Band 8 Focus

- Do not spend the whole essay on reasons and forget the judgement.
- Do not list many causes; develop two.
- If mixed, make the final direction clear: `largely positive` / `mostly negative`.
- Separate cause language from evaluation language.

## Useful Language

| Function | Patterns |
|---|---|
| Reason | `This is mainly because...`; `One factor behind this trend is...` |
| Social change | `changing lifestyles`; `greater mobility`; `economic pressure`; `career opportunities` |
| Evaluation | `beneficial overall`; `problematic in the long term`; `largely positive`; `mostly negative` |
| Concession | `This may create some difficulties, but...` |

## Quick Review

Body 1 = why it happens.
Body 2 = whether it is good or bad.
