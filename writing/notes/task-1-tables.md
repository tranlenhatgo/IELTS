# Task 1 Tables

Skill: Writing
Part: Task 1 table report
Source: forecast table questions

## Core Strategy

Turn the table into groups. Do not describe rows one by one.

| Table Type | Best Grouping |
|---|---|
| Two years | increased / decreased / stayed highest |
| Many categories, one year | highest / middle / lowest |
| Countries + categories | similar countries / opposite countries |
| Units, not percentages | compare values directly; do not use `share` unless it is a percentage |

## Structure

| Paragraph | Job | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase the table. | unit, group, years |
| Overview | State highest/lowest and main direction. | no full detail |
| Body 1 | Main/highest or increasing group. | strongest figures |
| Body 2 | Lower/decreasing or secondary group. | smaller figures |

## Two-Year Table Frame

Use this for tables like average annual distance by transport mode in 1997 and 2007.

Overview:

`Overall, [category] remained by far the highest figure and increased sharply, while most smaller categories declined.`

Body split:

| Body | Focus |
|---|---|
| Body 1 | Categories that rose, especially the dominant one. |
| Body 2 | Categories that fell or stayed minor. |

Useful patterns:

- `[A] rose from [X] to [Y], making it by far the largest category.`
- `[B] increased fourfold, from [X] to [Y].`
- `[C] declined from [X] to [Y].`
- `[D] remained a minor category, at only [X] in [year].`

## Table Language Control

| If The Table Shows | Use |
|---|---|
| miles / kilometres | `distance travelled`; `miles per year`; `average annual distance` |
| money | `spending`; `expenditure`; `cost` |
| percentages | `share`; `proportion`; `percentage` |
| raw numbers | `number`; `total`; `figure` |

## Traps

- Do not use `percentage` if the unit is miles, dollars, people, or tonnes.
- Do not write one sentence for every row.
- Do not use `accounted for` unless the data is a share of a total.
- Keep the unit visible in the introduction and first body paragraph.

## Quick Review

Find the dominant category first.
Then group the rest by increase, decrease, or size.
