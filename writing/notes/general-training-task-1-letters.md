# General Training Writing Task 1 Letters

IELTS General Training Task 1 is a letter. The goal is not to sound impressive. The goal is to answer the situation clearly, use the correct tone, and cover every bullet point.

## Time

- Plan: 2 to 3 minutes
- Write: 14 to 15 minutes
- Check: 2 to 3 minutes
- Target length: at least 150 words

## Tone

Formal:

- Use when writing to a manager, company, official office, landlord you do not know, or someone in authority.
- Avoid contractions like `I'm` and `can't`.
- Use polite phrases.

Semi-formal:

- Use when writing to someone you know in a serious situation, such as a teacher, colleague, or landlord you know.
- Polite but less distant than formal.

Informal:

- Use when writing to a friend or family member.
- Contractions and warmer language are acceptable.

## Structure

1. Opening greeting
2. Purpose sentence
3. Bullet point 1
4. Bullet point 2
5. Bullet point 3
6. Closing sentence
7. Sign-off

## Fast Checklist

- Did I use the correct tone?
- Did I cover all three bullet points?
- Is the purpose clear in the first paragraph?
- Are paragraphs separated logically?
- Did I use a suitable sign-off?
- Did I check grammar, spelling, and punctuation?

## Useful Openings

Formal:

- `I am writing to inquire about...`
- `I am writing to complain about...`
- `I am writing to request...`

Semi-formal:

- `I am writing to let you know that...`
- `I wanted to ask whether...`

Informal:

- `I hope you are doing well.`
- `I just wanted to tell you about...`

## Useful Closings

Formal:

- `I look forward to hearing from you.`
- `Thank you for your time and consideration.`

Semi-formal:

- `I hope we can discuss this soon.`

Informal:

- `Hope to see you soon.`
- `Write back when you can.`
