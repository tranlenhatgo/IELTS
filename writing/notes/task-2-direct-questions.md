# Task 2 Direct Questions

Skill: Writing
Part: Task 2 essay
Question type: direct question / two-part question

## Position

Answer the exact question. Do not force agree/disagree language if the prompt asks `what`, `why`, or `how`.

## Structure

| Paragraph | Job |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase topic + preview answer. |
| Body 1 | First direct answer. |
| Body 2 | Second direct answer or deeper explanation. |
| Conclusion | Summarize the answer directly. |

## Fixed Frames

Introduction:

`Although [background topic] is important, I believe [answer direction]. This is because [answer 1] and [answer 2].`

Body 1:

- `The first thing [topic] can show us is...`
- `This matters because...`
- `For example...`

Body 2:

- `Another important lesson is...`
- `Unlike [comparison], [topic] can...`
- `This helps people understand...`

Conclusion:

`In conclusion, [topic] can teach people about [answer 1] and [answer 2], which [comparison topic] may not fully explain.`

## Forecast Example: Arts vs Science

Question focus:

`What can the arts tell us about life that science and technology cannot?`

Good answer directions:

| Arts Can Show | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| human emotion | science can explain behaviour, but art makes people feel it |
| moral conflict | stories show difficult choices, not only facts |
| culture and identity | music, painting, and literature preserve how people see themselves |
| personal meaning | technology solves problems, but art explores purpose and experience |

## Band 8 Focus

- Do not attack science and technology; compare their limits.
- Use abstract nouns clearly: `emotion`, `identity`, `values`, `meaning`, `experience`.
- Avoid personal-only answers; talk about people, society, and culture.
- Each body paragraph should answer the question, not just describe the topic.

## Quick Review

Question word controls the essay.
If the prompt asks `what`, your topic sentences should answer `what`.
