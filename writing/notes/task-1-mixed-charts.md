# Task 1 Mixed Charts

Skill: Writing
Part: Task 1 mixed visuals
Source: forecast mixed chart questions

## Core Strategy

Each visual has a different job. Keep the report balanced, but do not force a direct comparison if the bases are different.

Example: world water distribution + water usage by country.

- Pie charts = global context.
- Bar chart = country comparison.

## Structure

| Paragraph | Job | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Name both visual types and the shared topic. | `distribution of... and use of...` |
| Overview | Give one main point from each visual. | biggest share + main country pattern |
| Body 1 | First visual. | distribution / composition |
| Body 2 | Second visual. | comparison across countries/categories |

## Overview Formula

`Overall, [main pattern in visual 1]. In terms of [visual 2], [main comparison pattern].`

Water example:

`Overall, salt water made up the vast majority of the world's water, while most fresh water was stored in ice and glaciers. In terms of water use, farming dominated in India and China, whereas industry was the main user in Russia.`

## Body Logic

| Visual | What To Do |
|---|---|
| Pie chart | Start with largest share, then minor shares. |
| Bar chart | Group countries/categories by similar pattern. |
| Table | Mention dominant figure, then grouped secondary figures. |
| Line graph | Describe trend groups, not every year. |

## Traps

- Do not add percentages from different visuals unless they share the same base.
- Do not spend equal detail on a small context pie chart and a complex bar chart.
- Do not call a static comparison a trend.
- Keep units separate if visuals use different measures.

## Quick Review

Intro = both visuals + shared topic.
Overview = one major point from each visual.
Bodies = one visual per paragraph unless the data clearly connects.
