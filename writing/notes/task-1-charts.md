# Writing Task 1 Charts

Skill: Writing
Task: Task 1 chart/report
Focus: grouping, body structure, chart vocabulary
Note: This is chart/report Task 1.

## Structure Rules

| Rule | Meaning | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Group by trend, not by time | Put similar lines/bars/categories together: rise group, fall group, stable group, highest/lowest group. | Stronger overview and less mechanical description. |
| Write cumulatively | Describe one object from the start to the end of the period before moving to the next object. | Avoid repeating the same object in both body paragraphs. |

## Intro Paraphrase

Goal: change the wording without changing the data meaning.

Formula:

`The [chart type] compare/illustrate the [percentage/proportion/share] of [item] [verb] by/in [groups] in [time].`

Example:

Question: `The charts below show the percentages of food and goods bought in supermarkets in European countries in 1998 and 2008.`

Intro: `The two bar charts compare the proportions of food and goods purchased from supermarkets in three European countries in 1998 and 2008.`

| Question Word | Safer Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| charts | two bar charts / line graphs / pie charts |
| show | compare / illustrate / present |
| percentages | proportions / shares |
| bought | purchased |
| in supermarkets | from supermarkets |

Rules:

- Keep years, countries, units, and categories accurate.
- Do not add trends in the introduction.
- Use `compare` when there are multiple groups, years, or categories.

## Survey Bar Charts

Use this when the chart reports survey answers, not changes over time.

Intro frame:

`The bar charts illustrate the results of a survey conducted in [year] about [aspect 1] and [aspect 2].`

Overview frame:

`Overall, the data show a clear preference/tendency for [main pattern], while [minor pattern] was much less common.`

Body split:

| Body | Focus |
|---|---|
| Body 1 | First survey question / aspect. |
| Body 2 | Second survey question / aspect. |

Useful language:

| Function | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Survey group | `respondents`; `responses`; `participants` |
| Most common | `the dominant choice`; `the largest segment`; `the most popular option` |
| Least common | `a marginal category`; `an uncommon response`; `the least selected option` |
| Majority | `just over half`; `the majority of respondents`; `around four-fifths` |
| Small groups | `less than 10% collectively`; `only a small minority`; `a negligible proportion` |
| Preference | `showed a preference for`; `favoured`; `were more likely to` |

Control:

- Do not force trend language if there is no time period.
- For survey charts, `people who chose X` is often clearer than `the figure for X`.
- If two charts show different questions, describe one question per body.

## Chart Subtypes From Forecasts

| Type | Best Body Split | Main Control |
|---|---|---|
| Dynamic line graph | rising / falling / stable lines | describe each line from start to end once |
| Static grouped bar chart | highest/lowest activities or age groups | do not use trend language without time |
| Dynamic pie charts | categories that rise / fall / stay stable | use `share`, not `number` |
| Non-time line graph | high-emission vs low-emission groups | write `as [X] increases`, not `over the period` |

Dynamic line graph frame:

`Overall, [A] remained the highest figure despite [movement], while [B] increased and [C] declined. [D] stayed comparatively low throughout.`

Static grouped bar chart frame:

`Overall, [category] was the most popular activity among [group], whereas [category] attracted the lowest participation. Age differences were most visible in [activity].`

Non-time line graph frame:

`Overall, [A] produced the highest emissions across most speeds, while [B] was consistently the lowest. Several vehicle types reached their lowest point at around [speed] before rising again.`

Controls:

- Time graph: `from 1991 to 2001`, `over the period`, `by the final year`.
- Static graph: `in 2018`, `among the three age groups`, `participation was highest`.
- Speed/relationship graph: `at 60 kph`, `as speed increased`, `emissions fell and then rose`.

## Body Logic

Weak split:

- Body 1: 2000 to 2010 for all categories
- Body 2: 2010 to 2020 for all categories

Risk: every category gets repeated twice.

Stronger split:

- Body 1: categories that increased or stayed high
- Body 2: categories that decreased, fluctuated, or stayed low

For each object, describe the full period once:

`A started at 20%, rose steadily to 45% in 2010, and then peaked at 60% by 2020.`

## Fixed Sentence Frames

Use these to avoid writing spoken-style sentences from zero. Replace only the bracketed parts.

| Function | Frame |
|---|---|
| Overview: main rise | `Overall, [A] and [B] saw the most significant increases, while [C] rose more modestly.` |
| Overview: opposite trends | `Overall, [A] increased markedly, whereas [B] followed the opposite pattern.` |
| Start + end | `[A] stood at [X] in [year], before rising/falling to [Y] by [year].` |
| Strong increase | `[A] recorded a sharp increase, rising from [X] to [Y].` |
| Small increase | `[A] saw only a slight rise, from [X] to [Y].` |
| Decrease | `[A] declined steadily, dropping from [X] to [Y].` |
| Double / triple | `The figure for [A] more than doubled/tripled, from [X] to [Y].` |
| Highest | `[A] had the highest figure, at [X].` |
| Lowest | `[A] remained the lowest category, at around [X].` |
| Comparison | `[A] was higher than [B], at [X] compared with [Y].` |
| Gap | `The gap between [A] and [B] widened/narrowed from [X] to [Y].` |
| Overtake | `[A] overtook [B] in [year], reaching [X].` |
| Two items | `[A] increased sharply in [item 1], while its figure for [item 2] rose more slowly.` |

Quick control:

- Write `the figure for [category]`, not `the buying of [category]`.
- Write `recorded/saw/experienced an increase`, not `have a jump`.
- Write `among the three countries/categories`, not `out of all 3 country`.
- Write `more than doubled`, not `the increase is more than double`.

## Vocabulary Bank

| Function | Words / Phrases |
|---|---|
| Approximation | approximately, around, about, roughly, nearly, almost, just over, just under, slightly more than, slightly less than |
| Increase | rise, increase, grow, climb, surge, jump, double, triple, reach, peak at |
| Decrease | fall, decrease, decline, drop, dip, plunge, halve, hit a low of, reach the lowest point, bottom out at |
| Stability | remain stable, stay constant, remain unchanged, level off, plateau |
| Fluctuation | fluctuate, vary, recover, rebound, fluctuate around |
| Degree | slightly, marginally, gradually, steadily, significantly, sharply, dramatically, rapidly, moderately |
| High points | peak, highest point, maximum, top the chart, rank first, account for the largest share |
| Low points | lowest point, minimum, account for the smallest share |
| Comparison | higher than, lower than, the same as, similar to, compared with, in contrast, whereas, while, respectively |
| Gap/change relation | gap widened, gap narrowed, overtook, outnumbered, exceeded, was surpassed by |

## Band 8 Sentence Patterns

- `A rose steadily from X to Y, before peaking at Z in 2020.`
- `B followed the opposite pattern, falling from X to Y over the same period.`
- `C fluctuated around X, with no clear upward or downward trend.`
- `By contrast, D remained the lowest figure throughout the period.`
- `The gap between A and B widened significantly after 2010.`

## Quick Rule

Overview = group trends.
Body = describe each object once, from start to finish.
