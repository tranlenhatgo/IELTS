# Task 2 Agree / Disagree: Partial Position

Skill: Writing
Part: Task 2 argumentative essay
Question type: `To what extent do you agree or disagree?`

## Position

Use this route when you agree with part of the statement but reject, limit, or qualify another part.

Core rule: partial agreement still needs a clear final position.

## Structure

| Paragraph | Job |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase topic + state both agree part and limit/disagree part. |
| Body 1 | Explain why the statement is partly reasonable. |
| Body 2 | Explain why the statement is limited / not always true. |
| Conclusion | Restate the controlled partial position. |

## Fixed Frames

Introduction:

`Many people argue that [topic]. While I agree that [agree part], I believe that [limit/disagree part].`

Body 1:

- `On the one hand, this view is reasonable because...`
- `A further reason is that...`

Body 2:

- `On the other hand, I do not think this is always true because...`
- `This is especially the case when...`

Conclusion:

`In conclusion, I partly agree that [topic]. However, I believe [final position] because [strongest reason].`

## Band 8 Focus

- Do not make both sides equal if the question asks for your opinion.
- Make the stronger side obvious by the end.
- Use contrast carefully: `while`, `however`, `nevertheless`, `this does not mean`.
- Avoid vague conclusions like `both sides have advantages`.

## Quick Review

Partial position = agree with X, but limit/reject Y.
Final judgement must still be clear.
